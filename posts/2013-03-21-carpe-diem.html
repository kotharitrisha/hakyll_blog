Knowledge

Posted in: philosophy.

Once, a few wise men appeared at the court of a rich king, and claimed that they are the wisest men on the planet. The king decided to test their wisdom, and asked them to compile a book with all the knowledge of the world.

After days of effort, the wise men reappeared and presented the book to the king.

The king asked them to condense it further to a chapter.

After weeks of toil, they appeared again at the court, proud of their accomplishments.

The king was still not impressed. He asked them to reduce it to a page.

Months of sweat and blood led to the wise men presenting the page containing all the wisdom of the world.

The king was happy (I mean, if you can get all the knowledge of the world in a page, you better be happy!) However, to prove that they were the wisest men on the planet, they had to do more than any man could do.

The king asked them to present all the knowledge of the world in a sentence.

The men were astonished. (After all, is he so lazy that he can’t even read a page!?)

They accepted the challenge, and after a few years, returned to the court.

They handed the king a paper containing the wisdom of the world:

“And this too shall pass.”
